Artificial intelligence (AI) is intelligence demonstrated by machines.
Machine learning is a subset of AI that enables systems to learn from data.
Deep learning is a subset of machine learning that uses neural networks with many layers.
Neural networks are inspired by the human brain and consist of interconnected nodes.
Natural language processing (NLP) is a field of AI focused on the interaction between computers and human language.
Large language models (LLMs) are a type of neural network trained on massive text datasets.
Transformers are the architecture behind most modern LLMs, introduced by Google researchers.
Reinforcement learning is a type of machine learning where an agent learns by interacting with an environment.
Computer vision is a field of AI that enables machines to interpret visual information from images and videos.
Knowledge graphs represent information as a network of entities and their relationships.
Ontologies define the concepts and relationships within a knowledge domain.
Embeddings are dense vector representations of words, sentences, or entities in a high-dimensional space.
Gradient descent is an optimization algorithm used to train machine learning models.
Backpropagation is the algorithm used to compute gradients in neural networks.
Transfer learning involves applying knowledge gained from one task to a different but related task.
